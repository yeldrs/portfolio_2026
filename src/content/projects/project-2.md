title: "Building an agile tool to design with ethics"
client: "Caisse des Dépôts et Consignations (CDC)"
description: "Designing a tangible, methodological toolkit to embed ethical, eco-design, and inclusive principles into major digital platform development across the organization."
role: "Study Conceptor & Solution Developer (UX Strategist)"
roleDescription: "Worked in a two-person team over six months, responsible for end-to-end ideation, deep research, concept selection, and prototyping of the final strategic tool. The concept was selected by the client for future development."
context: "The project was driven by the imperative to shift the organization's digital mindset from 'techno-vorous' (resource-intensive) to 'thoughtful' (sustainable and responsible), addressing new societal and regulatory challenges. Initial research revealed a critical lack of a common vocabulary for ethics, ecology, and inclusivity, hindering cross-functional adoption."
problem: "How can we create a cohesive, adaptive, and engaging methodological tool to harmonize ethical, eco-design, and inclusive principles across diverse project teams, translating abstract concepts into tangible, actionable steps?"
keyInsights:
  - "Decision-maker awareness needed to be raised and formalized."
  - "Reusing existing, functional elements is key to saving time, money, and energy (Frugal Design)."
  - "Learning through manipulation and hands-on interaction leads to far better knowledge retention than passive observation."

methodology: |
  Adopted a structured, phased approach:
  1. **Research & Definition:** Identified problems and solutions across Eco-Design, Inclusive Design, and Ethical Design pillars.
  2. **Concept Alignment:** Selected 'Architecture and Information Structure' as the core focus to create a centralized, synthesized reference model.
  3. **Prototyping:** Developed three concepts, ultimately selecting the modular **'Archipel' Design Tiles** for their potential to facilitate collaborative, hands-on learning.

# 🚨 CORRECTION CLÉ : Utilisation de l'opérateur de bloc littéral (|) pour éviter les erreurs de parsing
designConception: |
  The retained concept, **'Archipel,'** is a set of tangible, modular project construction tiles. It functions as a playful, simple, and adaptive aid for cross-functional project conception (covering Eco-design, Ethics, and Inclusivity).
  Each tile is color-coded by category (Ethics/Inclusive/Eco) and includes:
  * The specific **Notion and Task** to perform.
  * The **Responsible Stakeholder** (Project Manager, Designer, or Developer).
  * The **Priority and Timeline** for implementation. This structural design ensures clarity, accountability, and seamless integration into framing and monitoring scenarios.
  
delivery: "The concept was selected by the CDC and green-lit for subsequent internal development. I presented the study at the Nantes Digital Week and led a dedicated development workshop with the CDC's internal design team in Paris. Delivered a final, physical prototype (laser-cut tiles, graphic overlays, and self-adhesive backing) ready for scaling."
metrics: "The strategic success is measured by the tool's defined objectives:\n* **Project Framing:** Providing Project Managers with a concrete framework for launching sustainable projects.\n* **Learning & Retention:** Improving knowledge acquisition of sustainable design principles via hands-on manipulation.\n* **Tangible Planning:** Enabling the concrete planning and tracking of eco, inclusive, and ethical tasks within a project timeline."
