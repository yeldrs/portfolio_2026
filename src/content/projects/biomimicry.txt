title: "Biomimicry — Interactive Educational Experience"
client: "Museum of Natural History, Nantes"
description: "Biomimicry and gamification to introduce the concept of pioneer plants."
role: "Product Designer"
roleDescription: "I co-led the project from scientific mediation to physical production. My focus was on UX strategy and product prototyping using woodworking and laser engraving."
context: "The Museum of Natural History of Nantes needed to help visitors visualize the principle of local 'pioneer plants'. The challenge was to translate a complex biological process into a 5-minute interactive experience for a multi-generational audience."
problem: "How to make the complex biology of pioneer plants intuitive for children while staying scientifically accurate for adults in a high-traffic museum?"

keyInsights:
 - "Technical Risk: Standard Web-AR proved unstable on 60% of visitors' older devices, risking high frustration."
 - "Audience Data: 40% of users were children under 13, requiring a playful experience with zero-instruction interface."
 - "Cognitive Load: to attract visitors and inplement the concept into our physical environment, a physical 'anchor' was needed before adding the digital AR layer."

methodology: "
  Working in a team of four, we collaborated with a biology specialist to translate scientific field data into a functional product. 
  I led the 3D modeling and engineered the wooden totem stand as the primary physical touchpoint, while my teammates managed the AR integration with a developer. 
  Together, we conducted live user testing and on-site deployment for 100+ visitors."
designConception:
  paragraph: |
    To mitigate technical failure, we pivoted the strategy from 'bring your own device' to a dedicated hardware station. I engineered a robust wooden installation that acted as the AR trigger, ensuring 100% uptime.
delivery: "A fully functional interactive installation delivered to the Museum. The package included the physical structure (wood & laser-engraved), the AR software layer, and a post-event report detailing visitor engagement patterns and hardware maintenance protocols."
metrics: "Successfully engaged 100+ visitors in 2 days. Implemented a critical UX update overnight after Day 1. Maintained 0% technical downtime when switching to dedicated hardware."
