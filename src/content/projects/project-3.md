title: "Building an accessible solution for collaborative batchcooking."
client: "Personal Social Innovation Project"
description: "End-to-end design (UX/UI) of a digital platform tackling student food insecurity and social isolation, by turning underutilized kitchens into shared learning spaces."
role: "Product Designer & UX Strategist (End-to-End)"
roleDescription: "I led the entire Design and Research process, partnering closely with a Data Scientist. My job was to ensure the user experience came first, and that our project evolves towards building an economical model serving genuine human connection."
context: "The project was driven by a simple insight: students struggle to afford healthy, home-cooked food, and urban loneliness is rising. Our vision was to transform unused kitchens into low-cost hubs for social exchange and practical culinary learning, making real food accessible."
problem: "The core challenge wasn't technical, it was psychological. How do we design an experience that dramatically lowers the emotional barrier of inviting a stranger into a private home (a kitchen)? The interface had to feel safe, friendly, and trustworthy enough for the social exchange to happen."
keyInsights:
 - "Trust Overcomes Cost: The biggest hurdle wasn't saving money, but the emotional anxiety of a potentially awkward or unsafe social match. Design had to focus on reassurance first."
 - "Learning is the True Value: Users were often more motivated by the chance to learn a new skill or share a culture than by the pure financial saving. The UX needed to highlight the pedagogical opportunity."
 - "Data Must Be Human: Our algorithm couldn't just match addresses; it had to match affinities (shared values, cooking style, desire to teach/learn). The data had to serve the human connection."
methodology: |
 I ran a practical, two-stage Design process:
 1. Listening to People (Discovery): I conducted interviews to map the specific fears and social motivations of both Hosts and Cooks. This research became the non-negotiable foundation for every design choice.
 2. Strategic Arbitrage: I used the insights to define a strict scope: the initial prototype would only focus on validating the core Trust-Matching-Learning loop.
 3. Designing for Safety: I structured the screens so that safety elements (reviews, verifications) were prioritized over the transactional information.
designConception:
 paragraph: |
  Our biggest strategic design decision was to turn the Host Profile into a Social Trust Passport. The interface was intentionally designed to showcase the human element:
 listItems:
  - "Inverted Visual Hierarchy: Social proof (host bio, community reviews, shared values) was given more visual weight than logistical details (kitchen size)."
  - "Design to Drive Data Strategy: I worked with the Data Scientist to ensure our algorithm prioritized specific qualitative criteria we found in research, such as 'The Host's self-reported teaching style'. This guaranteed the technology served social affinity, not just proximity."
  - "Creating Connection: We used friendly language and visual cues to make the booking process feel like arranging a friendly meet-up, not a purely financial transaction."
delivery: "The project successfully delivered a Validated Concept and Experience Foundation. This process proved that the core user problem (building trust) was solvable through design, mitigating the risk of investing in development before product-market fit was clear.\n\nKey Deliverables: Low-Fidelity Prototype (Figma) of the critical matching/booking journey, Trust-Centric Information Architecture, and a comprehensive User Research Synthesis."
metrics: 
 - "Target Adoption KPI (Future Goal): Our ultimate goal is an H/C Match Rate > 75%, proving high platform efficiency."
 - "Validated Trust KPI (Design Impact): Qualitative tests on the prototype showed that 80% of users completed the profile review process feeling comfortable and willing to book, which validates our trust design."
 - "Social Value KPI (Future Goal): The design established a framework to measure future success based on real value, aiming for 90% of users reporting they learned a new cooking skill after their first session."
