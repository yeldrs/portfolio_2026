title: "Trust Design: Validating the Feasibility of a Collaborative Sharing Economy"
client: "Personal Social Innovation Project & Feasibility Study (Batchcooking EDM)"
description: "End-to-End (UX/UI) conception for a collaborative economy platform addressing student food insecurity by optimizing the use of underutilized urban kitchens."
role: "Product Designer & UX Strategist (End-to-End)"
roleDescription: "Pivotal role in a two-person team (with a Data Scientist). Responsible for user research, information architecture, strategic design decisions, and delivering the low-fidelity prototype, with a critical focus on **human trust and safety**."
context: "The project addresses the dual constraints of student precariousness and underutilized real estate assets (kitchens) in metropolitan areas. The imperative was to prove the model's social viability and resource efficiency, transforming a logistical constraint into a **social and economic opportunity**."
problem: "How can we design a secure sharing service that generates sufficient user trust (between Hosts and Cooks) to guarantee adoption, while defining a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) that minimizes legal risks and high moderation costs?"
keyInsights:
 - "**Trust Outweighs Price:** Research showed that the main adoption barrier was not cost or logistics, but the **emotional transaction of trust and safety**. The design had to function primarily as a risk-mitigation tool."
 - "**Risk-Driven Scoping:** The MVP scope needed to be strictly limited to validating core safety/matching mechanisms, avoiding unnecessary feature development until trust was proven."
 - "**Data-Informed UX:** Qualitative user criteria identified in research must directly feed the matching algorithm, ensuring **human affinity** is prioritized over mere geolocation."
methodology: |
 I adopted a structured, insight-driven Design Thinking process:
 1. **Discovery & Friction Mapping:** Conducted user interviews with target Cooks and Hosts to identify pain points, trust barriers, and the specific vocabulary of safety.
 2. **Strategic Definition:** Synthesized insights to firmly set the MVP scope, focusing the value proposition on a 'risk-free' experience.
 3. **Trust Mechanism Conception:** Developed the Information Architecture (IA) and wireflow, prioritizing Host profile transparency and verification steps.
 4. **Prototyping & Quali Validation:** Delivered low-fidelity mockups and conducted qualitative testing to validate the clarity of the critical booking flow and the perception of security.
